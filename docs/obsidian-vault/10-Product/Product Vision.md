# Product Vision

## What StopTheSlop Is

StopTheSlop is a universal intake, curation, and synthesis layer for `anything about AI`.

People come here to:

- share something they saw, tried, asked, learned, or made
- submit text, URLs, screenshots, documents, clips, or other source material
- ask questions and continue in an ongoing AI conversation
- see what the system can ground from their submission, the site graph, and the live web
- help the community graph accumulate claims, guides, questions, answers, and entity knowledge

## What StopTheSlop Is Not

StopTheSlop is not:

- a raw social feed of public posts
- a customer support portal
- a vendor escalation pipeline
- an internal enterprise reliability dashboard
- a product that requires rigid front-door taxonomy before someone can contribute

## Product Positioning

The product should feel closer to an AI-native knowledge refinery than a public issue wall.

The value comes from:

- low-friction multimodal intake
- source-aware graph updates
- aggregation of recurring claims across tools, tasks, and topics
- grounded answers that combine local graph state with live web evidence
- derived public surfaces that stay useful even when raw submissions remain private

## Primary Users

- engineers and operators tracking what tools are good or bad at
- power users who keep finding useful guides, edge cases, and workarounds
- builders evaluating whether a tool or model is worth adopting
- curious observers looking for recurring patterns in AI behavior
- researchers and curators who want structured claims instead of disconnected anecdotes

## Product Principles

- low friction beats completeness
- accept unstructured input first, structure it after ingestion
- preserve source provenance even when public output is derived
- keep raw user submissions private by default
- publish graph-derived artifacts instead of raw community posts
- preserve disagreement instead of collapsing it too early
- let one composer handle submission, asking, and exploration

## Near-Term Success Criteria

- people can submit anything about AI quickly
- each submission yields a useful grounded response
- the graph accumulates source-linked claims, guides, questions, and entities without hand-curated forms
- the home feed feels alive through derived artifacts rather than raw posts
- visitors can tell what tools, topics, and claims are rising without using a dedicated search page
